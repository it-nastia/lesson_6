6 spooky things you didn’t know about ghosts

Scientists say there is no evidence that ghosts really exist. But there are plenty of people who think ghosts are real. Check out these un-boo-lievable facts about spooky spirits.

Some believe there are different types of ghosts. Orbs for example, appear as floating balls of light. Poltergeists, or “noisy ghosts” can touch objects in the physical world. They like to make noise and stir up mischief. Other ghosts can appear as mist or fog. Another, called the funnel ghost, shows up as a blurry spot in photographs.

Ghost hunters use a variety of equipment to investigate haunted places. A pair of night vision goggles can help to see in the dark. An EMF metre can be used detect changes in electromagnetic fields. They might also use thermal imaging cameras. These show hot and cold spots that would normally be invisible to human eyes. Finally, audio recording gear is used to capture odd or spooky sounds.

Why does it seem that ghosts prefer nighttime? Some say that when fewer electronic devices are used, we're more likely to notice spirits in our homes. And in some folklore, they refer to the witching hour. That’s a time of night where ghosts and witches are believed to be more powerful.

What do some people claim to be the telltale signs of a ghost? Here's a list of six of the most common hints that there may be a ghost nearby: 1) the room gets cold, 2) door behave strangely, 3) doors open on their own, 4) you hear whispering, 5) creaking stairs and 6) unexplained smells.

The classic Christmas ghost story tells the tale of a grumpy rich man, Ebenezer Scrooge. Scrooge loves his money more than anything or anyone. But on Christmas Eve he is visited by three spirits. The Ghost of Christmas Past reminds him how happy he was as a young man. The Ghost of Christmas Present shows him what everyone thinks of him. And the Ghost of Christmas Yet To Come shows him what awaits him if he doesn’t start being nice. He awakens on Christmas Day a changed man. And tries to change his future by being a better person.

The Legend of Sleepy Hollow is a story by Washington Irving. In it, Ichabod Crane, a schoolmaster, attends a party in the town of Sleepy Hollow. As he rides home through the woods, he passes through several haunted places.
